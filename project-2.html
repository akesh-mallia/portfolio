Northstar Rocket

Wisconsin Space Program (WISP)

2026

Background

A liquid rocket powered by isopropyl alcohol and liquid oxygen. The rocket is 13.5 feet tall and 8 inches in diameter, with a thrust of 1000 lbf. This marks the first liquid rocket developed in the state of Wisconsin.

My contributions were largely in the ground support equipment (GSE) of the rocket, specifically in enabling the smooth fill operations of the vehicle.

Challenges

High Pressure

The high pressure ratings necessary for our fluid systems was a frequent challenge. In one instance, we had a major failure at cold flows because the fitting connecting to the COPV leaked, causing a massive drop in pressure and a physical jolt of the tank. To solution this, I did the simple thing of applying torque stripes to joints, and I got intimately familiar with the strengths and weaknesses of different fittings on the rocket, including compression and NPT.

Results

Liftoff!

In May 2026, Northstar achieved liftoff from the Mojave Desert. It was a collective effort to which I was proud to have contributed.

GSE Cart

I helped integrate a cart for GN2 fill operations, connecting the source (K-bottles) to the tank (COPV).

Pressure Modeling

Used Engineering Equation Solver (EES) to model pressure drop across pipes, hoses, and fittings in the rocket, allowing me to size an orifice intended to slow down COPV fill. Without the orifice, the COPV could have gotten extremely hot.
