NASA CubeSats

NASA Goddard Space Flight Center

2023-24

Background

Across two internships at the NASA Goddard Space Flight Center in the summers of 2023 and 2024, I worked on two CubeSat projects. These were proof-of-concept missions built with fellow interns. In the first summer, I was the payload engineer, working on a CubeSat intending to provide a third-person perspective for the OSAM-1 mission. The second summer, since I had some experience working on CubeSats, I became the team lead, guiding 11 other interns through a laser wireless power transfer CubeSat mission.

Challenges

Cross-Disciplinary Leadership

Working as a lead between subsystems was difficult, as it required enough knowledge to understand the blockers for each team. To overcome this, I ensured that I was available to talk to each of the teams and understand their process. I also tried to follow the NASA Systems Engineering Handbook as much as possible.

Results

Two completed flat-sats

A flat sat is a fully integrated satellite that has not yet been put into the form of a CubeSat. It allows benchtop testing of the various subsystems to be completed.

End-of-summer presentations

Presented to subject matter experts across the Engineering and Technology Directorate (ETD), as well as to high school students in the Space Summit DC (2023).
