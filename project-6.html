ME 201 Crane

University of Wisconsin-Madison

2024

Background

ME 201 is the introduction to mechanical engineering course, where throughout the semester, I worked on a crane project in a group of three. The crane needed to fit within size and weight (900 g) restrictions, and critically, it needed to lift 1.75 kg.

The design we picked leveraged a block-and-tackle pulley system for mechanical advantage. It was largely constructed of laser-cut acrylic sheets, as well as 3D-printed brackets. My contributions to the team were focused on manufacturing, assembly, and testing.

Challenges

Acrylic Snapping

One thing we did not consider when we used the pulleys was that as the string wound around them, it would ride up the pulley wall. This is because the string was not being fed perfectly in line with the pulley. After a certain point of riding up the wall, the string would fall back into the valley of the pulley, causing a jolt in the system. This jolt happened to send a shock down the boom arm and into the chassis, causing a major fracture! To fix this, I 3D printed a guide for the string such that it fed onto the center of the pulley, and I enlarged the cross-section of certain sections of the chassis.

Results

Successful lift of 1.75 kg during the class competition

We accomplished what we set out to do: lift the load for as little mass as we could.

Gained new skills from my first semester

Built CAD assembly in SOLIDWORKS, motor code in Arduino IDE, basic breadboard circuits, and Excel calculators of stability/lift time.
